Borders

With borders, you have a lot of options, most of which you will never use - when in doubt, assign a solid border. The other options are rare-use cases.

  • a dotted border
  • a dashed border
  • a solid border
  • a double border
  • a grooved border (the effect will depend on the border color)
  • a ridged border (the effect will depend on the border color)
  • an inset border (designed to look like a cutout)
  • an outset border (designed to look like a button)
  • no border
  • a hidden border (occupies the space of a border but is not visible)
  • you can also mix and match styles

Border-Radius

As mentioned in the previous Web page, you can also round the corners of your Web page elements by applying a border-radius property to them. You can apply radii to individual corners by naming them, or you can use a combined declaration in which you follow the border-radius property with 4 values representing the top-left, top-right, bottom-right, and bottom-left corners, in that order.
